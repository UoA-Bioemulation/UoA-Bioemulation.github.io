Centre for Bio-remulation
The University of Auckland

Remulation, or reverse emulation, is the process of designing and subsequently using the design of a physical plant to validate a controller. A plant describes a physical process such as the physics of a vehicle and the controller is a digital control program (such as the automotive cruise controller) to control the speed. In this research we are proposing the science of bio-remulation, where the plant is a biological process (say the electrical conduction of a human heart) and the controller is the associated digital controller (say the pacemaker). We argue that bio-remulation is a new idea that needs new science to ensure a systematic validation process for biomedical, implantable devices that are highly safety-critical. We argue that conventional bio-emulation of implantable devices using live organs is limiting similar to the limitations of clinical trials of drugs over human subjects.

The Centre for Bioremulation consists of joint work between the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department (ECE) and the Auckland Bioengineering Institute (ABI) at The University of Auckland in Auckland, New Zealand. We've collaborated with researchers from Indian Institute of Technology Bhubaneswar (IITBBS) in Bhubaneswar, India and from UC Berkeley in Berkeley, USA.
