Centre for Bio-remulation
The University of Auckland

Remulation, or reverse emulation, is the process of designing and subsequently using the design of a physical plant to validate a controller. A plant describes a physical process such as the physics of a vehicle and the controller is a digital control program (such as the automotive cruise controller) to control the speed. In this research we are proposing the science of bio-remulation, where the plant is a biological process (say the electrical conduction of a human heart) and the controller is the associated digital controller (say the pacemaker). We argue that bio-remulation is a new idea that needs new science to ensure a systematic validation process for biomedical, implantable devices that are highly safety-critical 1. We argue that conventional bio-emulation of implantable devices using live organs is limiting similar to the limitations of clinical trials of drugs over human subjects.
