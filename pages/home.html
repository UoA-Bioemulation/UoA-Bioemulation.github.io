BioEmulation™ Research Group
The University of Auckland

Emulation, is the process of designing and subsequently using the design of a physical plant to validate a controller. A plant describes a physical process such as the physics of a vehicle and the controller is a digital control program (such as the automotive cruise controller) to control the speed. In this research we are proposing the science of BioEmulation™, where the plant is a biological process (say the electrical conduction of a human heart) and the controller is the associated digital controller (say the pacemaker). We propose BioEmulation™ to validate medical devices such as Pacemakers and ICD devices.
